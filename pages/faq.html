    1. What types of bets can I make?

    • Our app, like other forms of betting, features three forms of betting for available games. These bets are the spread, the moneyline, and the over/under. Parlays and other forms of compounding bets are not currently supported on our platform.

    2. How do moneyline bets work?

    • The moneyline is simply a bet on who will win the game. If you placed a bet on a moneyline bet for a team to win, all you need to win the bet is for that team to win.

    3. How do spread bets work?

    • Spread bets look much like moneyline bets in that you pick sides based on a team except that it is not as much a pick for a team to win as it is a bet on what the difference in the two teamsâ€™ scores will be. This difference in scores is based on who is favored to win the game and is called the points spread. You can find the points spread to the right of home team. For more information on how to read the points spread, see the section on favored teams here.
    • In the associated example, the Pittsburgh Steelers are favored to win, and they are favored to win by 7.5 points. In order to win a spread bet on the Steelers, they would need to beat the Browns by 7.5 points. In order to win a spread bet on the Browns, they would need to not lose to the Steelers by more than 7.5 points. Since the final score of that game was Steelers 24 and Browns 9, spread bets on the Steelers would have won and spread bets on the Browns would have lost.

    4. How do over/under bets work?

    • Over/ under bets are guesses as to whether or not the combined scores of both teams will reach a certain value. The value they need to equal is indicated as a number next to the away team (the team listed on top in American sports). A bet for the over means that both teams need to score more (or over) than the indicated number in order to win the bet. A bet for the under means that both teams need to score less than (or under) the indicated number in order to win the bet.

    5. How do I know who is favored to win a game?

    • Usually one team is favored to win the game. This is indicated on the primary game screen as a number next to the home team (the bottom team in American sports) called the points spread or simply the spread. A negative spread means that the home team is favored to win by that many points. A positive spread means that the away team is favored to win by that many points.
    • There can be instances when the spread is zero. This just means that the teams are relatively closely matched and are considered to be equally likely to win the game.
    • In the associated example, the points spread is positive which means that the away team, the Pittsburgh Steelers, are favored.

    6. How do I know what my payout will be if I win?

    • The first thing to know about payouts is that the odds of a bet are used to determine how much money you will earn if you were to win a bet. The next thing to know is that odds are all based around $100. Also of note is that some of the numbers associated with odds are positive and some are negative.
    • If the odds of a bet are positive, then the odds are associated with a wager of $100, and the associated odds are the amount that you would win on that $100 wager. The total payout would then be the original wager plus the amount won. For example, the odds of the Cleveland Browns winning this game were +273. A win on a wager of $100 on this bet would have won $273 on top of your original $100 wager, so a win would have paid $373.
    • If the odds of a bet are negative, then the odds are associated with a win of $100, and the associated odds are the amount that you would have to wager to win $100. The total payout would still be the original wager plus the amount won. For example, the odds of the Cleveland Browns beating the spread, or losing by losing by less than 7.5 points, in this game were -110. To win $100 one would have to place a $110 wager on this bet. If the Browns would have beat the spread, then this bet would have won, and the bettor would have won $100 on top of their original wager of $110 for a total payout of $210.
